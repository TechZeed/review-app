# Brainstorm Results: Portable Individual Review App

**Date:** 2026-04-14
**Topic:** A review/reputation app for frontline individuals — portable, customer-given recognition that stays with the person across jobs and orgs
**Goal:** Full concept — product, positioning, monetization, differentiators
**Facilitator:** Elanchezian (Progressive Brainstorming)
**Participants:** Muthukumaran Navaneethakrishnan, Amara (Trend Research)

---

## Core Vision

**"Every individual is a brand."**

The person who actually serves the customer — the frontline worker — never gets the credit directly. Recognition flows to the org, the brand, the team... but the individual who made the experience great stays invisible. This app makes the individual sovereign — they own their reputation, the org is a guest, and the profile is portable forever.

**Key Principle:** When a user gets a review from a customer, that review will always be carrying with them even when they move out of the organisation.

---

## Theme 1: Core Identity — "Every Individual is a Brand"
*Ideas: #1, #2, #3c, #10, #12, #13, #34, #35, #36*

### The Problem
- **The Attribution Gap (#1):** Reviews go to the entity (Google review for "XYZ Dealership"), not the person ("Ramesh who helped me"). The individual vanishes into the org's rating.
- **The Proof Gap (#2):** No verifiable customer-sourced credential exists for individuals. Resume is self-reported. Manager reference is secondhand. The customer who experienced the value firsthand has no channel to vouch.
- **Customer Wants to Advocate (#3c):** Customers want to publicly back the *person*, not just the company. They need a place where saying "this human was exceptional" actually sticks to them permanently.

### The Solution
- **Anti-LinkedIn (#10):** Your profile is what *customers* say about you, not what *you* claim about yourself.
- **"Every Individual is a Brand" (#12):** The positioning anchor for the entire product.
- **Always-Carry QR (#13):** The individual's brand is always accessible, always portable — on their badge, phone, business card.
- **Individual is Sovereign (#34):** The individual creates the profile independently. An org can "tag" themselves ("Ramesh works at ABC Dealership"). When Ramesh leaves, he untags the org. Reviews, qualities, testimonials — all stay with him.
- **Network Effect on the Individual (#35):** Each review compounds. The more reviews, the more valuable the profile. They never leave.
- **Org is a Guest (#36):** The org tags in, benefits from visibility into their team's reputation. But the individual controls the profile. The org is a guest, not the owner.

## Theme 2: The Five Qualities Framework
*Ideas: #11, #14, #15, #16, #17, #18*

### Research Foundation
Based on the SERVQUAL/RATER model (Parasuraman, Zeithaml & Berry), the academic gold standard for service quality measurement, adapted for individual personal brands.

### The Five Qualities

| # | Quality | Customer Language | What It Means |
|---|---------|------------------|---------------|
| 1 | **Expertise** | "Expert in their domain" | Applied knowledge — not what you know, but what you can do |
| 2 | **Care** | "Made me feel valued" | Genuine attention, individualized treatment |
| 3 | **Delivery** | "Did exactly what they promised" | Reliability, follow-through, accuracy |
| 4 | **Initiative** | "Went beyond what I asked" | Proactive, extra-mile behavior |
| 5 | **Trust** | "I'd come back to this person" | The ultimate endorsement — personal loyalty |

### Cross-Industry Validation

| Role | Expertise | Care | Delivery | Initiative | Trust |
|------|-----------|------|----------|------------|-------|
| Car salesperson | Knows models, financing | Listened to my budget | Got me the deal promised | Found a better option I didn't ask for | I'd send my friend to them |
| Hotel concierge | Knows the city, restaurants | Remembered my preferences | Booked exactly what I wanted | Surprised me with an upgrade | I'd request them next visit |
| Bank RM | Knows products, regulations | Understood my financial anxiety | Processed my loan on time | Flagged a better rate proactively | I'd trust them with my savings |
| Nurse | Clinical competence | Made me feel safe | Administered care correctly | Checked on me after shift | I'd want them again |

### Design Principles
- **Universal across industries (#16):** Five qualities work for retail, hospitality, banking, healthcare, auto sales — no industry-specific dimensions needed.
- **Customer language, not HR language (#17):** Mapped to how customers naturally think, not how HR measures performance.

## Theme 3: The 10-Second Review Flow
*Ideas: #6, #19, #20, #21, #22, #23*

### The Flow

| Second | Action | Required? |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| 0-1 | Scan QR → profile loads | — |
| 1-3 | **Tap 1-2 qualities** that stood out | Yes (mandatory) |
| 3-5 | **Overall thumbs up** | Yes (mandatory) |
| 5-10 | **Pick one:** quick text / voice note / short video | Optional |

### Key Design Decisions
- **Personal QR Code (#6):** The individual owns the QR code — it's their identity, not the company's. Portable across jobs.
- **"Pick what stood out" (#20):** Customers highlight 1-2 qualities, not rate all five. No star ratings. No sliders. Just "what made this person special?"
- **Aggregation over time (#21):** Hundreds of customers voting reveals the person's signature strengths. The profile becomes a heat map of what customers love about you.
- **One tap mandatory, everything else optional (#22):** The speed principle. The moment you require two taps, completion drops off a cliff.

### Research Backing
- HappyOrNot smiley terminals: single tap, <1 second, 50%+ response rates (vs 2% for surveys)
- Uber model: one tap for stars, optional tag chips — progressive disclosure
- Emoji-based feedback: 70% higher response rates than text surveys

## Theme 4: Rich Media as Social Proof
*Ideas: #24, #25, #26*

### Three Expression Modes

| Mode | Effort | Impact | Fakeability |
|------|--------|--------|-------------|
| **Text** | Low — quick one-liner | Good — searchable, scannable | Easy to fake |
| **Voice** | Low — hold to record, 15s max | High — emotion, gratitude, authenticity | Hard to fake at scale |
| **Video** | Medium — selfie-style, 30s max | Highest — face + voice + emotion = personal endorsement | Nearly impossible to fake at scale |

### Why This Matters
- A written review says "Ramesh was great"
- A voice review says "Ramesh was great" — and you *hear* the genuine gratitude
- A video review says "Ramesh was great" — and you *see* the customer's face saying it

**A video on Ramesh's profile isn't a review. It's a personal endorsement.** No company can fake that. No resume can compete with it.

## Theme 5: Monetization — Two-Sided Marketplace
*Ideas: #27, #28, #29, #30, #31, #32, #33*

### Revenue Model

| Tier | Who Pays | What They Get | Price Signal |
|------|----------|---------------|--------------|
| **Free Forever** | Individual | QR code, profile, collect unlimited reviews, share publicly or keep private | $0 |
| **Pro Individual** | Individual (optional) | Analytics, downloadable reputation report, custom QR designs, video highlights reel | $5-10/month |
| **Employer Dashboard** | Employer/org | See their team's reviews, identify top performers, internal leaderboard, retention signals | $50-200/month per location |
| **Recruiter Access** | Recruiter/staffing agency | Search profiles by quality scores, industry, location. Contact top-rated individuals | $500-1,000/month per seat |
| **API/Data** | Enterprise HR tech | Integrate reputation scores into ATS, background checks | Custom pricing |

### Key Principles
- **Individual free, recruiter/employer pays (#27):** Two-sided marketplace — the value is in the verified profiles, recruiters pay to access.
- **Profile private by default (#28):** Individual controls visibility. Can be fully public, recruiter-visible only, or completely private.
- **Customer-verified reputation is the moat (#31):** LinkedIn has self-reported skills and colleague endorsements. You have actual customers saying "this person is exceptional at Care and Initiative." That's gold for recruiters hiring frontline roles.

### Competitive Pricing Context
- LinkedIn Recruiter: $10,800-$15,000/seat/year
- Triplebyte: $7,000+ per hire or 20-25% of first-year salary
- Glassdoor Enhanced Profiles: six-figure enterprise contracts
- Edge: employee recognition SaaS, undisclosed pricing

## Theme 6: Trust & Anti-Fraud — Five-Layer Verification Stack
*Ideas: #40, #41, #42, #43, #44, #45, #46*

### The Problem
If anyone can scan a QR and leave a fake review, the whole "customer-verified reputation" promise falls apart. This is existential for the app.

### Structural Advantage
Google/Yelp/Amazon's problem: anyone on the internet can leave a review with no proof of interaction. Your app's advantage: the review flow starts with a QR code scan in person.

### Five-Layer Anti-Fraud Stack

| Layer | Mechanism | What It Stops |
|-------|-----------|---------------|
| **Layer 1: QR Scan** | Captures timestamp, location (if allowed), device fingerprint. Proof of physical presence. | 90% of fake attacks — no drive-by internet reviews |
| **Layer 2: Phone OTP** | OTP to customer's phone. One phone = one review per individual per time window (e.g., once per week) | "Scan my own QR 50 times" attack |
| **Layer 3: Time-Window Token** | QR scan generates review token valid for 24-48 hours. Can't stockpile tokens. | Historical fake reviews, review farming |
| **Layer 4: AI Pattern Detection** | Flag same device reviewing multiple individuals rapidly. Flag location clusters. Flag identical quality picks. Flag velocity spikes. | Coordinated fake review campaigns |
| **Layer 5: Video/Voice Trust Amplifier** | Video reviews nearly impossible to fake at scale. Voice carries authenticity markers. Profiles with video get "Verified Testimonial" badge. | Bot-generated reviews, AI-written fakes |

### The Killer Insight
You don't need to be perfect. You need to be better than LinkedIn endorsements (one-click, zero verification) and Google reviews (no proof of interaction). Your bar is already higher on day one because of the QR scan requirement.

### Additional Trust Layer
- **Potential employer can contact the customer (#47):** If the customer opts in, a potential employer can verify the review directly with the customer. This turns a review into a verifiable reference.

## Theme 7: Verifiable References — The Killer Feature
*Ideas: #47, #48, #49, #50*

### How It Works
The customer, when leaving a review, can optionally say: **"I'm willing to be contacted to verify this."** They don't have to. But if they do — that review gets a special "Verifiable" badge.

### The Impact

| Traditional Hiring | Your App |
|-------------------|----------|
| "Give me 3 references" → ex-managers, hand-picked | 12 real customers who opted in to vouch |
| Reference calls feel rehearsed | Customer references are raw and genuine |
| References are static — same 3 people forever | New references accumulate over time |
| No way to verify the reference is real | QR scan + location + timestamp + customer consent = verified chain |

### Verified Chain
QR scan → timestamp → location → phone verification → customer consent = **unbreakable proof of genuine interaction and endorsement.**

## Theme 8: Competitive Moat & Positioning
*Ideas: #7, #8, #9, #37, #38, #39*

### Competitive Landscape

| What Exists | What's Missing |
|-------------|----------------|
| Google reviews → business | Reviews → the person |
| Edge → internal recognition | Portable recognition that travels |
| RateMyAgent → one industry | Cross-industry individual profiles |
| GigCV → gig workers only | All frontline workers — retail, hospitality, banking, healthcare |
| LinkedIn recommendations → from colleagues | Recommendations from customers |

### Key Competitors & Lessons

| Competitor | What They Prove | Their Gap |
|-----------|----------------|-----------|
| **Edge** (startedge.com) | 130k+ employees, $4M+ earned. Customers will attribute reviews to individuals when prompted. | Recognition isn't portable. Employee leaves, reviews stay with the org. |
| **RateMyAgent** | Entire industry shifted to individual-level reviews. Customers *will* review individuals. | Vertical-locked to real estate only. |
| **GigCV / My Digital Backpack / Yoma** | Portable reputation is an emerging concept with real demand. | Early-stage, focused on gig economy only. Traditional frontline workers unserved. |
| **LinkedIn** | 930M profiles, professional identity standard. | Self-reported skills, colleague endorsements. Zero customer-sourced proof. |

### Defensibility — Three Layers

1. **Network effect on the individual:** Every review makes the profile more valuable. Different graph than LinkedIn (customer proof vs. professional connections), different lock-in.
2. **The org is a guest, not the owner:** Philosophically opposite to Edge, Glassdoor, every enterprise tool. Hard for big tech to copy because their business model requires owning the data.
3. **Focus moat:** Too niche for LinkedIn (930M professionals across every function). Too human for Google (algorithmic, not personal). Too small for Apple. Sam Altman principle: even a billion-dollar idea on Tim Cook's desk gets ignored — everyone's busy with their own thing.

### Growth Strategy
Step-by-step growth, not billion-dollar vision. Focus on a narrow market, make something users love, then expand. Niche problems rarely have markets large enough to maintain the attention of incumbents.
